New Dale-Chall Test

The New Dale-Chall readability test calculates the U.S. grade level of a document based on sentence length and the number of unfamiliar words. Unfamiliar words are words that do not appear on a list of common words that are familiar to most 4th-grade students. In addition, numerals and proper nouns are considered familiar and not included in the overall unfamiliar word count.

The original familiar word list was only 763 words; however, Professors Dale and Chall extended this list to 3,000 in 1995. Note that Readability Studio uses this revised version.

The New Dale-Chall test looks up its result from a chart and returns a U.S. grade range (e.g., "4" or "7-8"). This is an important difference from other tests that use formulas to calculate floating-point precision grade levels.

Because this formula is based on the usage of familiar words (rather than syllable or letter counts), it is often regarded as a more accurate test for younger readers.
