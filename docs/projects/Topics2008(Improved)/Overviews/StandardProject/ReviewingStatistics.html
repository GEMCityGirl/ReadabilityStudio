Reviewing the Statistics

After opening a project, click on the Statistics icon on the project sidebar to view all the document's statistics, which include the following:

  • Number of paragraphs and average number of sentences per paragraph
  • Number of sentences and average number of words per sentence
  • Number and percentage of difficult sentences
  • Number of words
  • Number of syllables and average number of syllables per word
  • Number of characters and average number of characters per word
  • Number and percentage of numeral words
  • Number and percentage of monosyllabic words and number of unique monosyllabic words
  • Number and percentage of 3+ syllable words and number of unique 3+ syllable words
  • Number and percentage of hard SMOG words and number of unique hard SMOG words
  • Number and percentage of hard Fog words and number of unique hard Fog words
  • Number and percentage of 6+ letter words and number of unique 6+ letter words
  • Number and percentage of Dale-Chall unfamiliar words and number of unique Dale-Chall unfamiliar words
  • Number and percentage of Spache unfamiliar words and number of unique Spache unfamiliar words
  • Number and percentage of EFLAW miniwords and number of unique EFLAW miniwords

Statistics for any custom familiar-word tests will also be included in this window if your statistics level of detail is set to medium or verbose.

A "Notes" section may be included at the bottom of this window if there are any items of interest detected (e.g., a high sentence-length average). If your document requires improvement, then this section is usually a good place to begin.

Note that 3+ syllable words and Fog and SMOG hard words are all the same except for how numerals (i.e., phone numbers and dates) are handled. SMOG always sounds out each digit of a numeric word, whereas Fog entirely ignores numeric words. You have control over how numeric words are handled from the Document Analysis area of the Options dialog (available from the Tools menu).

This window can be printed or exported.
