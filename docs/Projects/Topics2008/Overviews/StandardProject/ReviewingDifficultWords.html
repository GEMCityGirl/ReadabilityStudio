Reviewing the Difficult Words List

After opening a project, click on the Difficult Words icon in project sidebar to view the lists of difficult words.

The types of difficult-word list that may be shown (depending on which ones were selected from the Project Wizard) are:

3+ syllable words: This list displays all of the words in the document that are three or more syllables. It also displays how many syllables each word contains and their respective frequency counts.

6+ character words: This list displays all of the words in the document that are six or more characters. It also displays how many characters each word contains and their respective frequency counts.

DC unfamiliar words: This list displays all of the words in the document that are not in the Dale-Chall familiar words list and their respective frequency counts. The Dale-Chall word list is a collection of words that are considered to be familiar to most fourth grade readers. Words in the document that are not found on this list may be less familiar to younger readers and hence more difficult.

Spache unfamiliar words: This list displays all of the words in the document that are not in the Spache familiar words list and their respective frequency counts. The Spache word list is a collection of words that are considered to be familiar to most primary-age readers. Words in the document that are not found on this list may be less familiar to younger readers and hence more difficult.

All words from the document: This list displays all of the words from the document and their respective frequency counts.

Note that if you are ignoring incomplete sentences, then these lists will only include the words from complete sentences. If you need to review all of the words from the document, then select the option Include in analysis and treat as full sentences. Please refer here to learn more about how Readability Studio handles incomplete sentences.

To view any of these lists, simply click on the tab strip:

To find the most complex words, sort the Syllable count column of the 3+ syllable words list. You can sort any of the columns by clicking on the column header. To change the sorting order to either ascending or descending, click the column header a second time. As you can see below, the words with the most syllables are now shown at the top of the list.

If your test scores are indicating that the document is too difficult for your target audience, then you should review overly complex or long words. You may consider seeing if any shorter synonyms could be used in their place. The same type of review can also be performed with the 6+ character words list by sorting the Letter count column.

To help find the most difficult words for the Dale-Chall list, select the Dale-Chall tab and sort the Frequency Count column. The most frequently occurring words are the ones that should be replaced with more familiar synonyms that most readers would recognize.

To find a specific word in a list, go to the Edit menu and select the Find... option. You can also select the list and begin typing the word and the selection will move to the word (if it is in the list).

Any of these lists can be printed or exported if you want to further review them outside of Readability Studio. To print a list, go to the File menu and select the Print... option. To save a list, go to the File menu and select the Export hard word list... option. You can also find these options on the toolbar or by right clicking on a list.
