SMOG Test

The SMOG readability formula calculates the U.S. grade level of a document based on sentence length and number of complex words*.

The McLaughlin SMOG is generally appropriate for secondary age** readers. SMOG tests for 100% comprehension, whereas most formulas test for around 50%-75% comprehension.

Note that numerals are fully syllabized (i.e., sounded out) for this test, so the program overrides your numeral syllabication setting when calculating it.

The SMOG Formula

L = ROUND(√W) + 3

Where:

L U.S. grade level
W Number of complex words (3+ syllables)

This test requires a 10-sentence sample, with 3 samples being recommended for best results. Note that Readability Studio analyzes your entire document and does not use subsamples for its test calculations. If a formula requires a subsample of a specific size, then standardization is used.

Note that this test is often referred to as an acronym for “Simple Measure of Gobbledygook”. However, the origin of its name is actually a nod to Robert Gunning's Fog index.

* 4th grade to college level.

* Words that contain three or more syllables.
